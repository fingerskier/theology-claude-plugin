# Numerology Skill

Given a number (e.g., 7, 12, 40, 153), research its biblical and theological significance by answering the following questions:
* What is the core symbolic and theological meaning of this number in Scripture?
* What are the number's factors and multiples, and do those component numbers carry their own theological weight?
* Where does this number first appear in the Bible, and what does that context establish?
* What are the most significant occurrences of this number across the canon?
* What biblical metaphors, images, and symbols are associated with this number?
* Are there gematria traditions or Hebrew/Greek letter-number connections relevant to this number?
* How does this number relate to other theologically significant numbers?

## Output Format

Produce content under the heading `## Biblical Numerology: {number}`.

Organize answers as `### ` subsections:

### 1. Theological Significance
The core symbolic meaning of this number in Scripture. What theological concept does it represent? Include the Hebrew and/or Greek word for the number with its root. Trace how this meaning developed across biblical literature — is it consistent, or does it shift between contexts?

### 2. Factors and Multiples
Mathematical decomposition and its theological resonance. Use a factors table:
| Factor | Symbolic Meaning | Composite Significance |
| --- | --- | --- |

For multiples, show how the number scales (e.g., 7 x 10 = 70 elders; 12 x 12 = 144; 40 x 10 = 400 years). Note where factors or multiples appear together in Scripture to reinforce meaning.

### 3. First Mention
Apply the principle of first mention: identify the first occurrence of this number in the biblical text (Genesis forward). Quote or closely paraphrase the verse. Explain how this initial context sets the trajectory for the number's symbolic use throughout Scripture.

### 4. Key Occurrences
A table of significant appearances across both Testaments:
| Passage | Context | Thematic Significance |
| --- | --- | --- |

Include at least 8-12 occurrences spanning OT and NT where possible. Group by thematic pattern if helpful.

### 5. Metaphorical and Symbolic Associations
How does this number interact with other biblical symbols, imagery, and metaphors? Examples: 7 appears with lampstands, seals, trumpets, bowls; 12 with tribes, apostles, gates, foundations; 3 with divine appearances, denials, days. Show the number functioning within larger symbolic systems (tabernacle dimensions, temple measurements, apocalyptic visions, genealogical patterns).

### 6. Gematria and Hebrew/Greek Number Values
Where relevant: Hebrew letter-number equivalences (each Hebrew letter has a numeric value), Greek isopsephy, and any significant gematria traditions in Jewish or early Christian interpretation. Note which traditions are ancient and well-attested vs. speculative. Flag any gematria claims that lack scholarly consensus.

### 7. Cross-Number Connections
Relationships to other theologically significant numbers. Use a connections table:
| Related Number | Relationship | Scriptural Example |
| --- | --- | --- |

Show how this number participates in numerical patterns across Scripture (e.g., 3-7-12-40 as a set of completeness numbers; 6-666 as falling short of 7; 8 as new beginning following 7).

### Formatting
- Hebrew/Greek: **term** (Hebrew: script, *transliteration*)
- Strong's numbers: H1234 / G5678
- Biblical citations: Book Chapter:Verses
- Verse references: (v.1), (vv.1-5)
- Factor/occurrence/connection tables with `| --- | --- | --- |` separators
- Distinguish between well-attested scholarly interpretations and speculative or devotional traditions
- Tone: scholar addressing an amateur audience; confessional/evangelical, no particular tradition
